How the pricing logic works

A quick reference for any staff member using this tool for the first time

1. Direct costs are built up first

Facilitator, assessor and moderator fees (day rate × number of days), travel (distance × rate × trips), accommodation and subsistence (if the delivery is off-site and overnight), venue hire, and every per-learner cost (materials, PPE, SETA registration, EISA, certification, insurance) are added together into a Subtotal Direct Cost.

2. Hidden and consequential costs are added automatically

A contingency buffer covers things that go wrong on real programmes: learner no-shows, re-assessments, weather delays, last-minute venue changes. A cost-of-capital charge covers the fact that Tiespro pays facilitators, travel and materials up front, often before the client pays or before any SETA grant is reimbursed. A compliance/accreditation overhead covers the ongoing cost of keeping TETA accreditation current — audits, POE storage, QMS maintenance — spread across every paying programme.

3. Company overhead and target margin are applied

A company overhead allocation (office, admin salaries, software, insurance) is added, then the target gross margin is applied to arrive at the List Price. The target margin defaults to 40% specifically so that even a full 25% discount still leaves a healthy margin — see the live indicator change colour as you move the discount slider.

4. Revision days are additional, not part of the standard programme

Revision Days (pre-EISA exam prep) are costed the same way as training days but kept in their own line, both internally and on the client quote, and the discount slider never applies to them. This keeps the standard programme fee comparable across quotes and makes clear to the client that revision support is a value-add, not a hidden cost buried in the base price.

5. Training/assessment/moderation day defaults are sourced from SAQA credits

Selecting a qualification pre-fills days based on that qualification's registered SAQA credit value (see the Curriculum Day Reference table under Rate Assumptions). QCTO curricula define credits and outcomes, not a fixed day-count — the pre-filled numbers are Tiespro's own scheduling design and should be checked against the current registered learning programme, especially for Taxi Driver where a public credit record could not be confirmed.

6. The discount slider never lets you quote a loss silently

Green = margin at or above the minimum floor (default 20%). Amber = margin positive but below the floor, or discount above 15% — requires manager approval before the quote can be generated. Red = the quote is at or below cost — blocked entirely, reprice before continuing.

7. Manager approval

When a quote needs approval, click "Submit for Manager Approval" — this saves the quote as Pending. A signed-in manager reviews it from the Quote Tracker and approves or rejects with a comment. "Generate Client Quote" stays disabled until an approval is granted (or the quote is repriced into the green).

8. Quote Tracker & follow-up

Once a quote is sent, set its status to "Sent" and a follow-up date in the Quote Tracker. Overdue follow-ups (a follow-up date in the past with no resolution) are highlighted in red so nothing slips through.

9. Generating the client quote

The client-facing quote only ever shows packaged line items (Training Delivery, Assessment & Moderation, Examination/EISA, Travel & Logistics, Materials & Compliance, Total) — it never reveals your internal cost breakdown or margin. Click "Generate Client Quote" to preview and print/save as PDF using your browser's print dialog.

Keep this current: travel and subsistence rates are tied to SARS-published figures that change every tax year (1 March). Facilitator/assessor/EISA fees should be checked against your actual signed contractor and AQP agreements at least twice a year.
